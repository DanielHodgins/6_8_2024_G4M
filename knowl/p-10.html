Two core concepts of Git are commits (illustrated in Figure 1.1.1 by circles) and branches (illustrated in Figure 1.1.1 by lines). A commit represents the state of of your project at a particular point in its history. Branches allow this history to not be linear: you can branch off to experiment on a particular new feature, then merge this “feature branch” back into the “main branch” when it’s complete. This is particularly useful when multiple people collaborate (Chapter 6) on a Git-managed project.
